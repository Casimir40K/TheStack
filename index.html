    The Protocol
    Master System v7.0 — reference guide
    Hard Stops
    Combinations that damage the barrier — never do these
    ✕
    No retinol + face tan drops (same evening)
    Retinol sensitises skin; applying tan on top causes irritation and uneven colour. These two never share a night.
    ✕
    No salicylic acid + shave (same evening)
    Acid on freshly shaved skin = active on a compromised barrier. Wednesday's shave was moved to AM precisely to allow PM salicylic acid.
    ✕
    No retinol + shave (same evening)
    Same barrier-risk logic as acid + shave. Monday shave is afternoon, retinol is Thursday/Sunday PM — no overlap.
    ✕
    No salicylic acid within ~24h of face tan drops
    BHA strips the skin surface and fades tan colour. Wednesday PM salicylic → Thursday is fine; Friday tan is far enough away.
    ✕
    One strong lane per night only
    Strong lanes: retinol, salicylic acid, face tan drops, shave stress. Only one per evening. Copper peptides and niacinamide are low-stress but still avoid stacking with strong actives.
    Active Schedule
    Each active is locked to a specific night
    ○
    Retinol → Thursday PM + Sunday PM
    Applied to dry skin only. After cleansing, wait 5–10 min until fully dry before applying.
    ○
    Salicylic acid → Wednesday PM
    T-zone only for the first 2–3 weeks. Targets sebaceous filaments. Apply after double cleanse.
    ○
    Face tan drops → Friday PM
    After the shave → moisturise → 2–3 hour wait sequence. Apply in an even layer. No oil cleanse before tan unless heavily sweaty.
    ○
    Copper peptides → Monday PM
    In the PM double-cleanse ritual. Kept separate from the afternoon post-shave azelaic/cicaplast session.
    ○
    Niacinamide → Tuesday PM + Saturday PM
    Reinforcement and hydration support. Low-stress, compatible with both body tan night (Tue) and recovery from Saturday's pull session.
    Key Sequences
    Order and timing within a session
    →
    Retinol nights: cleanse → wait → apply
    Oil cleanse → foaming cleanse → wait 5–10 min until skin is fully dry → retinol → moisturise. Damp skin increases retinol penetration and irritation risk.
    →
    Friday tan sequence: shave → moisturise → wait 2–3h → tan
    Afternoon: foaming cleanse → shave → rinse → moisturise. Then wait at least 2–3 hours. Evening: optional light foaming cleanse if needed → face tan drops in even layer.
    →
    Monday post-shave (afternoon): cleanse → shave → rinse → azelaic → cicaplast
    Post-shave acids go in the afternoon slot. Copper peptides come later in the PM double-cleanse routine — these are two distinct sessions, not one.
    →
    Wednesday AM shave sequence: cleanse → shave → rinse → azelaic → moisturise → Vit C → SPF
    Shave moved to AM to clear the PM slot for salicylic acid. Full morning routine follows the shave.
    Cleansing Protocol
    When to double cleanse and when not to
    ○
    Standard PM: oil cleanse → foaming cleanse
    Double cleanse is the default on all skincare nights. Oil cleanse removes SPF and sebum; foaming cleanse clears remaining residue before active application.
    ✕
    Friday tan night: skip oil cleanse
    Unless heavily sweaty, skip the oil cleanse before face tan drops. Stripping the skin too much before tan risks patchy or faded colour. Foaming cleanse only if needed.
    Body Care Timeline
    Tan and exfoliation scheduling
    ○
    Body gradual tan → Tuesday PM
    Apply lotion evenly after the PM double cleanse. Even application is critical to avoid streaking.
    ○
    Body exfoliation → Thursday AM
    Dove beauty bar + African net sponge in the morning shower. Exfoliation preps the skin surface to hold tan colour more evenly.
    ○
    Body scrub → Saturday AM
    Pineapple body scrub for smoother skin and scent. Avoid Dove bar the day after body tanning — Sunday may be day-after-tan depending on timing. Keep as per v7.0.
